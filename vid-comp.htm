Music

Music is a universal art form that expresses emotions, culture, and identity through sound, rhythm, and melody. Genres categorize music based on shared characteristics like instrumentation, rhythm, harmony, and cultural origin—such as classical, jazz, rock, pop, hip-hop, and electronic—each offering unique styles and emotional experiences.

POP

Pop music is defined by its broad appeal, catchy melodies, and commercial accessibility. It is not a fixed style but an evolving genre that absorbs elements from rock, R&B, hip-hop, electronic, Latin, and global sounds to stay current.

Hip-hop

Rap is a vocal technique and music genre rooted in rhythmic speech, lyrical storytelling, and social commentary. Originating in the 1970s in the Bronx, New York City, it emerged from African-American and Caribbean communities as a core element of hip-hop culture, alongside DJing, breakdancing, and graffiti

Rock

Rock music originated in the 1940s–1950s in the United States as rock and roll, evolving from African-American musical traditions like blues, rhythm and blues, and gospel, while also incorporating country, folk, and jazz influences. It is typically built around the electric guitar, bass, drums, and vocals, often in a 4/4 time signature with a strong backbeat
